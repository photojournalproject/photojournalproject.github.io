Through the lens of everyday life, the Photo Journalism Project explores the power of gratitude. The uploaded photos display numerous emotions, appreciation, and a unique vision of the world. The photographs aim is to allow individuals to recognize the inherent beauty in their own special moments.

30.06.2023r.

A river near my home

I found this place while taking a tram back home. I was amazed by how quiet and close to my house it was at the same time. I promised myself to visit again, and took this photograph, which is my current wallpaper. I've actually came to the location on several occasions since then. I would sit there and reflect for hours, while looking at the lake.

I took this picture with a cheap camera, so it needed some color correction to improve its' quality. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.

This photograph was acceptable to begin with, but I used my color correction skills to make it appear more detailed. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.

23.08.2023r.

Abandoned factory

This picture was a gem that I discovered on Google Maps during summer. It was an abandoned synthetic gasoline factory, however, due to the efforts of a local organisation, the buildings were in good condition. Me and my friend decided to go to explore the roof of the main facility from where the views were breathtaking. It was an extraordinary day.

The middle part of the picture was quite dark and monotonous, so I made it lighter and more detailed. Moreover, I sectioned my friend out and made her more visible. Finally, I made similar adjustments to the building in the background as well. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.

09.09.2023r.

Sunrise by the river

During the weekend, my friend and I decided that we should make the most of our free time. That's why we decided to get up early, around 6 o'clock and see the breathtaking sunrise overlooking a nearby lake. My friend was covered in sun streaks so beautifully, that I had the urge to capture it in an image.

Unfortunately, my friend was not visible in the original photograph, which prompted me to edit it. By framing and lightening, I successfully made her the main element of the picture. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.

02.11.2023r.

An autumn bouquet

In November I walked through my grandparents' village and came across a huge field of autumn flowers. I was so impressed with nature's beauty, that I decided to gather some of the flowers and create a bouquet. I thought it'd be a shame not to share this little artwork with my loved ones, so I took a picture of it.

To add an extra touch of loveliness to the image, I enhanced the colours of the autumn flowers and removed my hand from the picture. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.

04.11.2023r.

Really sweet orange cat

When I was in my grandparents' village, my cat was being taken care of by my neighbour. I knew he was in good hands, but I missed him very much. Then I met this sweet orange cat on the street. I stopped to watch him and he was licking himself for 30 minutes! I thought he was really adorable and I took this picture to show to my friend, who is an absolute cat lover.

This photo came out a little bit too dark, so I had to lighten it and apply some color correction. The original can be seen by hovering the mouse over the photo.
